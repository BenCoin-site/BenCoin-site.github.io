How to Buy BEN Token: Step-by-Step DEX Guide

  1. Set up a self-custody Ethereum wallet — MetaMask, Rabby, or any EVM-compatible wallet. Never buy meme coins on a platform that doesn't give you control of your private keys.
  2. Fund your wallet with ETH — buy ETH on a reputable centralised exchange (Coinbase, Kraken, Binance) and withdraw to your personal wallet address.
  3. Obtain the official BEN contract address — this is the most critical step. Get the contract address from the official ben.eth community accounts or the BEN token page on Etherscan. Do not use a contract address shared in a DM or unofficial source.
  4. Go to Uniswap — navigate to app.uniswap.org directly (bookmarked URL). Connect your wallet and select "Swap".
  5. Paste the BEN contract address into the token search field — do not search by name alone as multiple fake tokens can share the name "BEN". Confirm the token logo and contract address match the official one.
  6. Set slippage tolerance — meme coins require higher slippage than blue-chip tokens due to price volatility. 1–5% slippage is typical; very small or illiquid pools may require higher. Do not set slippage above 10% as it exposes you to excessive MEV sandwich attacks.
  7. Enter the ETH amount and review the output — check the BEN amount you'll receive, the price impact, and the gas cost before confirming.
  8. Confirm the swap — sign the transaction in your wallet. Once confirmed on-chain, BEN will appear in your wallet. You may need to add the BEN contract address to your wallet's token list manually if it doesn't appear automatically.
Price impact warning: Meme coin pools are often shallow relative to their market cap. A trade representing even 1–2% of the pool's total liquidity can move the price significantly against you. Use a swap aggregator (1inch, Paraswap) for larger purchases to find the best execution across all available liquidity.

Best Practices for BEN Holders and Traders

  • Only invest what you can lose entirely — this is not a figure of speech for meme coins. The realistic worst case is zero. Size your position accordingly — most experienced meme coin traders allocate no more than 1–3% of their portfolio to any single community token.
  • Verify the contract address every single time — bookmark the Etherscan page for the verified BEN contract address. Never copy a contract address from social media, DMs, or group chats without cross-referencing the official source.

Troubleshooting BenCoin: Token Not Showing, Wrong Token, Failed Swaps

"BEN doesn't appear in my wallet after purchase"

  • BEN is an ERC-20 token that requires manual import in most wallets. Go to MetaMask → Import Tokens → paste the verified BEN contract address. The token will appear with your balance once imported.
  • Verify the purchase transaction on Etherscan first — confirm the swap succeeded and ERC-20 tokens were transferred to your address. If the transaction failed, no BEN was received (and your ETH minus gas remains in your wallet).

"I think I bought the wrong BEN token"

  • Check the contract address of the token in your wallet on Etherscan. Compare it character-by-character with the verified official BEN contract address. Even a single character difference means it's a different (likely worthless fake) token.
  • If you bought a fake token, the ETH is spent and unlikely to be recoverable. This is why verifying contract addresses before every purchase is non-negotiable.

"My BEN swap transaction failed"

  • Check the failure reason on Etherscan — common causes for meme coin swap failures: slippage too low (price moved beyond your tolerance during block confirmation), gas limit too low, or the token having a sell/buy tax that exceeds your slippage setting.
  • If the token has a transaction tax (e.g. 5% buy tax), your slippage must exceed that tax percentage plus normal price movement. Set slippage to at least the tax % + 1%.
Etherscan is ground truth: For any BEN transaction question, the Etherscan record of your wallet's ERC-20 transfers is definitive. What your wallet UI displays may lag or show incorrectly — the on-chain record does not.
